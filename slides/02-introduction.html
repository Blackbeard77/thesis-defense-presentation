Introduction

Motivation

Dialog systems are a fundamental component of modern video games — shaping interaction between players and NPCs, and directly influencing how narratives are delivered and experienced.

  • Player expectations for immersive, reactive NPCs are rising
  • Dialog must satisfy both narrative goals and technical constraints
  • Traditional solutions are struggling to keep up with scale
  • New AI approaches introduce exciting — but untested — possibilities
The Gap

Most existing work focuses on narrative quality or immersion — very little addresses the technical feasibility of integrating these systems in real game development.
This Thesis

Contributes to filling the gap with a developer-oriented technical comparison built inside a real Unity prototype.
Ashraf Lehawany · Thesis Defense · Supervisors: Prof. Dr. Andreas Jäger & Christoph Hahn · 26.03.2026 Introduction · Motivation

Problem Definition

DIALOG TREES
Predictable Cheap

Rigid Hard to scale

Manual authoring effort grows exponentially as game complexity increases.

LOCAL LLM
Private No API cost

High hardware Complex setup

Offers independence and customization but demands substantial computational resources.

CLOUD LLM
Powerful Easy access

Pay-per-use Network dep.

Highly capable but introduces recurring costs and reliance on external providers.

💡 The core problem: Developers — especially in small studios — have no structured framework to decide which approach fits their project's resource, performance, and design constraints.

Research Question

This thesis is guided by the following central research question:

"What technical potentials and limitations arise when implementing NPC dialog systems using traditional dialog trees, local large language models (LLMs), or cloud-based AI models- and how do these approaches compare in terms of feasibility, performance, and scalability within a Unity prototype environment?"
